Factor models — CAPM vs Fama-French

Reading the loadings: Mkt (SPY) is the S&P 500 beta — and the CAPM row's alpha is exactly the "Alpha vs SPY" figure at the top of the page. SMB (small minus big) > 0 means the portfolio tilts toward small caps; HML (high minus low book-to-market) > 0 means a value tilt, < 0 growth; RMW > 0 tilts toward profitable firms; CMA > 0 toward conservative investors (low asset growth). The punchline: watch what happens to alpha as factors are added — return that looked like skill under CAPM often turns out to be compensation for factor exposure (Bodie ch. 7, 18). A |t| above ~2 is conventionally "statistically significant"; below that, treat the estimate as noise.

Notes & definitions

Beta measures how much the portfolio moves with the market — a beta of 1.1 means the portfolio tends to move 1.1% when the benchmark moves 1% (Bodie ch. 7). Alpha is the return left over after accounting for market exposure: positive alpha means the portfolio beat what its beta alone would predict (ch. 18). Sharpe ratio is excess return per unit of total risk (ch. 5). Max drawdown is the worst peak-to-trough loss over the period. All statistics come from daily returns over the trailing year, annualized; alpha and beta from an OLS regression of portfolio excess returns on benchmark excess returns.

Notes

Hindsight caveat: this replays history with today's portfolio and a position it didn't hold — it estimates the risk characteristics a position would add, not the return to expect. The lesson: a volatile stock can still lower portfolio risk if its correlation is low; a "safe" stock adds risk if it moves with what you already own (Bodie ch. 6). The marginal-impact question — "what does this add to this portfolio?" — is the right frame for the class vote.

Questions worth asking

Did accepted picks beat rejected ones? If not, the class vote added no information. Were the sells right? Selling well is harder than buying well; loss aversion makes classes hold losers (Bodie ch. 9). Hit rate vs magnitude: a 40% hit rate can still win if the winners are big — read both.
